Scientific Method

  1. Observation: Observe something that you want to understand or want to know more about. Ex. leaves are green

  2. Question: Make a question about the observation. Ex. Why are leaves green

  3. Hypothesis: Create a testable statement or prediction about the question. Ex. Leaves are green because some pigment in them doesn’t absorb green

  4. Prediction: State what you think will happen if the hypothesis is correct. Ex. Leaves are green because the chlorophyll in them doesn’t absorb green

  5. Experiment: Design and began a test to gather data to test the hypothesis. Ex. Test what chemicals in leaves reflect green light .

  6. Analysis: Analyze the data to see if it supports or disproves the hypothesis. Ex. The chemical that gives leaves the green look is chlorophyll thus proving the hypothesis.

  7. Conclusion: Make conclusions based on the data, and revise the hypothesis or ask new questions if needed. Ex. Chlorophyll gives off the green look to leaves without it they are red-brown.

For more information go check out https://www.fs.usda.gov/detail/r2/home/?cid=stelprdb5388915 and remember the seven steps in the scientific method.
